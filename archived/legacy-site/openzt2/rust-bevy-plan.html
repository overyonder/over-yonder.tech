OpenZT2 Bevy Plan

OpenZT2 is a greenfield, native 64-bit Bevy zoo-management game. It must look and behave as though it was designed for Bevy ECS from the beginning: live state belongs to entities, small components, focused resources, typed assets, and narrow parallel systems. There is no compatibility host, shadow runtime, manager graph, duplicate world, or Rust-shaped copy of the recovered C++ program. The C++ oracle and binary investigation tell us what Zoo Tycoon 2 did; they never dictate the architecture of the new game.

The game is designed for as close to bare-metal speed as possible and pulls its assets from a high-speed binary layout of our own choosing. Imagine that the preflight converter is another team responsible for making its output meet OUR contract for speed. The Bevy game specifies the flat CPU tables, GPU-upload-ready payloads, streaming blocks, stable identifiers, and direct lookup layout it wants. Preflight parses the original archives and documents, resolves enabled DLC and mod overrides, and produces that finished native content. The game only validates and loads it; source parsing, conversion, decompression into intermediate models, and pack-precedence logic are forbidden in the engine.

Build the complete playable game in player-visible slices: first a buildable zoo, then living animals, an operating zoo, persistence, progression, expansion systems, and final authenticity closure. Recover missing behavior just in time, implement it directly in the correct Bevy owners, and delete wrong or duplicate architecture instead of preserving it with adapters. Required improvements over the original are controller support, much larger mod capacity, and redo alongside undo. Validate in bulk at playable boundaries, with performance and bounded memory treated as product requirements rather than late optimization work.
