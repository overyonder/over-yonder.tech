# Buy the Tulip

<div class="author-badge">
  <img src="https://github.com/KaiStarkk.png?size=64" alt="Kieran Hannigan" />
  <a href="https://github.com/KaiStarkk">Kieran Hannigan</a>
</div>

*Buy the Tulip* takes the cleanest classroom version of asset pricing and gives
it the rudest possible test: Dutch tulip prices, VOC shares, a seventeenth-century
dike bond, and an investor sitting down on 25 November 1636, ten weeks before
the crash. The model says buy. The point is not that tulip mania was secretly
reasonable; it is that a tidy model can be internally correct and still escort
you, with excellent manners, into a canal.
